About

Dr. Sarah Johnson is a Professor of Egyptology at the University of Cairo with over 20 years of field experience. Her research focuses on New Kingdom temple architecture, epigraphic studies, and the religious practices of ancient Egypt.

She has directed excavations at Karnak, Luxor, and the Valley of the Kings, and has published extensively on temple construction techniques and the symbolic geography of Theban religious complexes.
